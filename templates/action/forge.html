Automated Shortcodes

You can use shortcodes to automatically insert data from a parent monster. Note: this only occurs when the item is attached to a Giffyglyph monstersâ€”shortcodes are otherwise left as-is.

Randomised Dice Rolls

Insert randomised dice rolls with a special shortcode. You can use any dice size, as shown below:

[12, d4] = 4d4 + 2

[25, d12] = 3d12 + 6

[66, d30] = 4d30 + 4

Shortcodes

Use shortcodes to pull data out of a Giffyglyph monster and insert it into your text. There are currently 24 shortcodes for you to try:

[level] [attackBonus] [damage] [damageDie] [ac] [hpMax] [dcPrimaryBonus] [strMod] [strSave] [dexMod] [dexSave] [conMod] [conSave] [intMod] [intSave] [wisMod] [wisSave] [chaMod] [chaSave] [xp] [proficiency] [cr]

Shortcode Modifiers

Use mathematical modifiers with your shortcodes to transform the final number and apply specific rounding. For example:

[level * 2] = Monster Level x 2

[damage + 4] = Attack Damage + 4

[damage + 4] = Attack Damage + 4

[(hp / 3) + 10] = (Hit Points / 3) + 10

[round(damage / 4)] = Attack Damage / 4, rounded

[ceil(damage / 4)] = Attack Damage / 4, rounded up

[floor(damage / 4)] = Attack Damage / 4, rounded down

Shortcodes and Dice

Add a dice value to a shortcodes to transform it into a dice roll. For example:

[damage, d8] = Attack Damage (shown in d8)

[12 + str-mod, d20] = 12 + Strength Mod (shown in d20)

[10 * level, d6] = 10 * Level (shown in d6)

[damage, damageDie] = Attack Damage (shown in the default die for the monster)

Scaling Attacks

When paired with a Giffyglyph monster, a scaling attack will automatically take into account the monster's proficiency bonus and the attack's relevant ability mod.

Use these to easily create features and attacks that work at every monster level.
